No. The model's weights are set before you ever open a conversation. Nothing you type can alter them. Your inputs are processed to generate a response and then discarded at the end of the session unless you or your administrator explicitly configure storage or fine-tuning separately.
Each conversation starts with a blank context window. Because nothing from previous sessions is passed in, Claude has no record of past exchanges. The next section covers context windows in detail and explains how to work around this for ongoing projects.
The model itself does not improve with use – the weights are the same on your first conversation as on your thousandth. What does improve is how you prompt it. Better context, clearer instructions, and structured inputs produce noticeably better outputs, which is what the remaining sections of this guide are about.
